Occupant · est. 2025

A research desk for the AI compute market.

Ron Bronson runs Occupant from a one-person studio. The work is published; the methodology is open; the desk takes no clients.

01 — Premise

AI compute is a market with no agreed-upon yardstick.

Prices move weekly. The basket of models shifts. Most public commentary leans on point-in-time anecdotes from whichever lab shipped last. There is no Bloomberg terminal for inference and there probably should not be one, but there is room for a small standing benchmark anyone can audit.

Occupant builds that benchmark in the open. Three indices, published daily, free, with the calculations visible end to end.

02 — The work

Three standing inquiries, each framed as a question the desk is testing.

Compute CPI asks whether AI compute deflates on a Moore’s-law cadence or compounds like rent. AEAI asks what AI usage looks like once you stop counting headlines — tokens, spend, energy. LDI asks the harder question underneath both: at what unit cost does a machine become cheaper than a person.

Each is treated as a hypothesis, not a product. The page is the experiment notebook.

03 — How it operates

One person, written rules, no sales motion.

Vendor-independent. No referral fees, no paid placement, no consulting attached. Index values publish as JSON. Price discrepancies above ten percent trigger a manual review before they reach the page.

The desk is staffed by one person. That constraint sets the cadence and the bar: nothing ships that cannot be defended in writing.
