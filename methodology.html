Methodology

How we calculate the Compute CPI. A market-basket approach modeled on the Consumer Price Index, adapted for AI inference costs.

Index Design

The Compute CPI synthesizes publicly available pricing data into a volume-weighted basket across capability tiers. We cross-validate across sources and flag discrepancies before publication.

Index Composition Weighting
$CPI Composite of all tiers Volume-weighted
$BULK Budget-tier models (<$1/MTok) Equal-weighted basket
$FRONT Frontier capability models Top-5 by Arena ELO
$JUDGE Reasoning-intensive workloads o1/o3/R1 class models
$LCTX Long-context workloads 128K+ context models

Build Cost Indices ($START, $AGENT, $THRU) apply fixed workload mixes to current tier prices, enabling organizations to track costs relevant to their specific use patterns.

Calculation

The index expresses current prices relative to a February 2025 baseline of 100. This allows institutions to track percentage changes in AI costs over time, independent of absolute dollar amounts.

Base Period
All subindices normalized to February 2025
= 100
Update Frequency
Index values published daily by 09:00 UTC
Daily

An index value of 87.2 means prices have fallen 12.8% from the baseline. A value of 112 would indicate a 12% increase.

Spreads

We track two key spreads that reveal structural dynamics in the AI pricing market:

Cognition Premium
