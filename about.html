About Occupant

Decision infrastructure for AI systems.

Who We Are

Occupant owns the judgment layer for AI systems. We measure costs, architect decision infrastructure, and help institutions govern autonomous decisions.

We operate across three integrated layers: Measurement (tracking AI costs and market activity), Interpretation (advising institutions on procurement and governance), and Infrastructure (building systems that make autonomous decisions legible and governable).

We've built working trust infrastructure for social platforms (Glowrm, PBJ, Leafroll) managing cross-application reputation, resource allocation, and accountability. The architectural patterns proven in social systems—decision receipts, judgment routing, authority chains, escalation triggers—apply directly to AI governance.

The Stack

Measurement

Compute CPI tracks price deflation across AI model tiers. AI Activity Index (AEAI) measures economic volume—token throughput, spend, and energy consumption. Updated daily from 2,300+ models. Public and free.

These indices prove we understand the terrain. The measurement layer demonstrates the depth of market knowledge required to advise on procurement decisions that hold up under scrutiny.

Infrastructure

Working systems governing decisions where identity travels and behavior matters:

  • Glowrm — Cross-platform trust infrastructure. API at drydown.replit.app. Portable reputation (20-80 scale) travels between applications. Good behavior on one platform carries forward. Reputation follows users across contexts.
  • SPA (Stratified Preference Allocation) — Methodology transforming infinite, low-cost actions into finite, high-cost decisions. Every action carries measurable opportunity cost. Tested across dating (PBJ), professional networking (Leafroll), and roommate matching (Roomies).
  • Judgment Routing — Decision architecture for AI systems. Stateful middleware evaluating agent proposals against uncertainty, stakes, authority, and novelty. Every decision produces a receipt. Authority chains stay explicit. Escalation paths remain legible.

Systems design determines who bears costs and how costs distribute. Making invisible costs legible enables governance and accountability across social platforms and AI systems.

Methodology

Compute CPI aggregates pricing data into a volume-weighted basket across four workload tiers. AEAI tracks economic activity through token volumes (60%), inferred spend (30%), and energy consumption (10%). Cross-validated across sources. Discrepancies flagged before publication. January 2025 = 100 for both indices.

Index Composition Weighting
$CPI Composite of all tiers Volume-weighted
$AIU Token volumes, spend, energy 60/30/10 weighted basket
$BULK Budget-tier models (<$1/MTok) Equal-weighted basket
$FRONT Frontier capability models Top-5 by Arena ELO
$JUDGE Reasoning-intensive workloads o1/o3/R1 class models

Build Cost Indices ($START, $AGENT, $THRU) apply fixed workload mixes to current tier prices. Track costs relevant to your specific use patterns.
