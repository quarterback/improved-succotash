About Occupant

Decision infrastructure for AI systems.

Who We Are

Occupant provides decision infrastructure for AI systems. In a world of agentic velocity, someone has to own the judgment layer—measuring it, architecting it, helping institutions govern it.

We operate across three integrated layers: Measurement (tracking AI costs and market activity), Interpretation (advising institutions on procurement and governance), and Infrastructure (building the systems that make autonomous decisions legible and governable).

This isn't theory. We've built working trust infrastructure for social platforms (Glowrm, PBJ, Leafroll) that manages cross-application reputation, resource allocation, and accountability. The same architectural patterns that make portable identity work for social systems apply to AI: decision receipts, judgment routing, authority chains, and escalation triggers.

The Stack

Measurement

Compute CPI tracks price deflation across AI model tiers. AI Activity Index (AEAI) measures economic volume—token throughput, spend, and energy consumption. Updated daily from 2,300+ models. Public and free.

The indices aren't the product. They're proof we understand the terrain well enough to advise you on procurement decisions that will hold up under scrutiny.

Infrastructure

We've built working systems for governing decisions in environments where identity is portable and behavior should have consequences:

  • Glowrm — Cross-platform trust infrastructure. API at drydown.replit.app. Portable reputation (20-80 scale) that travels between applications. Good behavior on one platform means you start with that reputation on another. Bad actors can't platform-hop to escape consequences.
  • SPA (Stratified Preference Allocation) — Methodology that transforms infinite, low-cost actions into finite, high-cost decisions. Every action has measurable opportunity cost. Tested across dating (PBJ), professional networking (Leafroll), and roommate matching (Roomies).
  • Judgment Routing — Decision architecture for AI systems. Stateful middleware that evaluates agent proposals against uncertainty, stakes, authority, and novelty. Every decision produces a receipt. Authority chains are explicit. Escalation paths are legible.

The throughline: Systems design determines who bears costs and how those costs are distributed. Making invisible costs legible enables better governance and accountability—whether for social platforms or AI systems.

Methodology

Compute CPI aggregates pricing data into a volume-weighted basket across four workload tiers. AEAI tracks economic activity through token volumes (60%), inferred spend (30%), and energy consumption (10%). Cross-validated across sources. Discrepancies flagged before publication. January 2025 = 100 for both indices.

Index Composition Weighting
$CPI Composite of all tiers Volume-weighted
$AIU Token volumes, spend, energy 60/30/10 weighted basket
$BULK Budget-tier models (<$1/MTok) Equal-weighted basket
$FRONT Frontier capability models Top-5 by Arena ELO
$JUDGE Reasoning-intensive workloads o1/o3/R1 class models

Build Cost Indices ($START, $AGENT, $THRU) apply fixed workload mixes to current tier prices. Track costs relevant to your specific use patterns.
