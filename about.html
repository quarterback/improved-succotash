About Occupant Index

The inflation rate for AI work

What We Do

We publish the inflation rate for AI work.

Occupant Index tracks the changing cost of representative AI workloads. We express this as an index (like CPI) and an inflation rate, updated daily.

When prices fall, the index falls. When they rise, it rises. February 2025 = 100.

Why It Matters

AI inference costs are dropping—but how fast? And for which workloads? Different model tiers (budget vs frontier vs reasoning) deflate at different rates. Different use cases see different cost pressure.

Occupant provides a standard reference for AI cost trends. Like the Consumer Price Index tracks household goods, we track AI compute.

What We Track

  • $CPI — The main Compute Price Index
  • $JUDGE — Reasoning-intensive workloads
  • $LCTX — Long-context workloads
  • $BULK — Budget tier (high-volume, low-cost)
  • $FRONT — Frontier tier (best capability)

We also publish spreads (premium for capability), exchange rates (relative token costs), and persona-based indices for different team profiles.

Independence

Occupant does not accept referral fees, sponsored rankings, or payments from model providers. We don't sell access or recommendations.

We track. We don't sell.
