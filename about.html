About Occupant

A public benchmark for AI compute, by Ron Bronson.

What this is

Occupant publishes three free, daily-updated indexes for AI compute: Compute CPI (a price index across model tiers), AEAI (an activity index for token volume, spend, and energy), and LDI (a labor displacement tracker for US federal workloads).

The motivation is simple: AI compute prices move every week, the basket of available models shifts constantly, and most public commentary on the AI market relies on point-in-time anecdotes. A standing benchmark — with a fixed methodology anyone can audit — makes the conversation comparable across time.

Methodology, source list, and update cadence are all on this page.

The indexes

Compute CPI

A volume-weighted basket of inference prices across four workload tiers (frontier, reasoning-intensive, long-context, budget). Tracks deflation in $/MTok over time. January 2025 = 100.

AEAI — AI Activity Index

A composite of three signals: token throughput (60%), inferred spend (30%), and energy consumption (10%). Designed to read like an economic activity gauge for the AI sector rather than a stock-price proxy. February 2025 = 100.

LDI — Labor Displacement Index

A per-workload tracker for the cost and staffing gap between human and AI labor across nine US federal workloads. Three pillars: human cost (BLS OEWS wage × loading factor), AI cost (Compute CPI basket), and substitution proxy (FPDS/USAspending contractor spend + AI procurement). Absorption classification is derived from OPM FedScope FTE counts. Structural gap and realized substitution are kept as distinct signals, not averaged.

Operating principles

  • Vendor independence. No referral fees, no paid placement.
  • Public data. All index values published as JSON.
  • Auditable methodology. Calculations are open and reviewable.
  • Free. Indices stay free for researchers, journalists, and operators.

Methodology

Compute CPI aggregates pricing data into a volume-weighted basket across four workload tiers. AEAI tracks economic activity through token volumes (60%), inferred spend (30%), and energy consumption (10%). LDI resolves each of nine federal workloads into a cost-per-unit comparison (BLS wage × loading vs. Compute CPI inference cost), pairs it with an FPDS-derived substitution proxy, and classifies the staffing response from OPM FedScope FTE deltas. Cross-validated across sources. Discrepancies flagged before publication. January 2025 = 100 for CPI and AEAI.

Data Access

All indexes are public and free. Daily values publish by 09:00 UTC. Full historical data available via JSON API. Commercial users, government agencies, and researchers welcome.
