Gov AI Benchmarks: Why Independent Pricing Matters

February 2026
A collaboration between Occupant and State Capacity AI

The Problem: Flying Blind in AI Procurement

Government agencies are signing AI contracts worth millions—often without knowing if they're paying fair market rates. Unlike traditional IT procurement, AI pricing lacks transparency. Vendors bundle per-seat fees, token costs, and "premium features" into opaque packages. The result? Agencies risk overpaying by 2x–3x, locking into unfavorable terms, or worse, deploying underpowered models because the "enterprise" tier was the only option on the table.

This isn't just about cost. It's about mission risk. Overpaying for AI diverts funds from other critical programs. Underpaying can mean unreliable models that fail when it matters most. And without benchmarks, there's no way to tell the difference until it's too late.

Why This Collaboration Matters

Occupant brings the Compute CPI and market-based pricing instruments to measure the cost of AI work. State Capacity AI brings deep expertise in government AI deployment and the policy levers that shape procurement. Together, we're publishing the first independent, use-case-specific benchmarks for government AI contracts.

For the first time, procurement teams can answer:

  • Are we paying $0.61 or $1.50 per million tokens for basic summarization?
  • Is $80/seat/month for "Enterprise AI" a good deal—or a ripoff?
  • What should we expect to pay in Year 2, when the "introductory pricing" expires?

These benchmarks aren't theoretical. They're derived from real market data (OpenRouter, LiteLLM, pricepertoken.com) and government-specific use cases, from form processing to complex policy analysis.

What's in the Benchmarks

1. Per-Seat Pricing by Use Case

Use Case Gov Benchmark Rate Typical Monthly Cost Negotiation Guidance
Light Use $5–15/seat Basic queries, forms Above $20? Push back.
Standard Use $25–40/seat Daily drafting, analysis $45+? Demand justification.
Heavy Use $50–80/seat Analysts, developers Above $100? You're paying for branding.
Enterprise $80–120/seat Full suite + reasoning Lock in multi-year or walk.

2. Per-Token Rates by Model Class

Model Class Gov Benchmark Rate Negotiation Guidance
Basic AI $0.61/MTok Above $1.00? Overpaying.
Standard AI $5.32/MTok Above $7.00? Unjustified.
Advanced AI $10.04/MTok Above $15.00? Needs premium features.
Reasoning AI $7.92/MTok Above $12.00? Get benchmarks.

1 MTok ≈ 750,000 words. A typical query uses 2,000–5,000 tokens (~$0.01–0.025 at benchmark rates).

3. Red Flags for Vendors

  • Won't disclose which models power the service.
  • "Unlimited" plans with hidden fair-use clauses.
  • Year 2+ pricing not disclosed upfront.
  • Charges for "AI processing" as a separate line item.

4. Questions to Ask Vendors

  • What's your per-token or per-query pricing? (Not just per-seat.)
  • Which models power the service? What's their Arena ELO?
  • Is pricing locked for the contract term, or variable?
  • What happens after Year 1?
  • Do you charge for failed queries or retries?
  • Can we audit actual usage vs. billed usage?

Why This Is a Big Deal

No More Surprises. Agencies can now compare vendor quotes to market rates before signing. No more "take it or leave it" contracts.

Better Negotiation Leverage

Armed with benchmarks, procurement teams can:

  • Push back on inflated pricing.
  • Lock in multi-year discounts.
  • Avoid "premium" upsells for basic features.

Future-Proofing

AI pricing is volatile. Benchmarks help agencies anticipate Year 2+ costs and build flexibility into contracts.

Accountability

Vendors know agencies are watching. Transparent pricing reduces rent-seeking and aligns incentives with performance.

How to Use This

For Procurement Teams

  • Use the benchmarks to set price ceilings in RFPs.
  • Require vendors to justify deviations from market rates.
  • Audit contracts against the Price Reasonableness Worksheet.

For Vendors

  • Align pricing with benchmarks to win trust and contracts.
  • Be prepared to explain model quality, data residency, and FedRAMP compliance.

For Oversight

  • Use benchmarks to flag outliers in agency spending.
  • Track trends over time to spot market manipulation.

The Bigger Picture

This isn't just about saving money. It's about repricing institutional reality. When government pays fair rates for AI, it:

  • Frees up funds for other critical programs.
  • Encourages competition, driving better tools at lower costs.
  • Ensures AI deployments are sustainable, not just flashy pilot projects.

AI procurement shouldn't be a black box. With these benchmarks, it doesn't have to be.
