Glowrm — Cross-Platform Trust Infrastructure

API: drydown.replit.app →

Portable reputation and trust scoring for systems where identity travels between applications. When someone behaves well on one platform, they start with that reputation on another. Bad actors can't platform-hop to escape consequences.

CROSS-PLATFORM REPUTATION EXAMPLE
Sarah Chen's Profile:
├── Leafroll (professional): 72 reputation
├── PBJ (dating): 68 reputation
├── Roomies (roommate finder): 70 reputation
├── Average across apps: 70
└── Status: Active (good standing)
If Sarah misbehaves on PBJ:
├── PBJ reputation drops to 45
├── Average drops to 62
├── Leafroll sees the change immediately
├── Future credit allocations reduced
└── Warning flags appear on profile

Key insight: Portable identity needs portable reputation. Good behavior should travel with you. Bad actors shouldn't get to reset by switching apps.

SPA — Stratified Preference Allocation

The foundational methodology: transform infinite, low-cost actions into finite, high-cost decisions. Every action has measurable opportunity cost. Tested across dating, professional networking, and roommate matching.

15 roster slots per week
Tiered signals (Firsts/Seconds/Thirds)

10 introduction credits per month
Variable cost (1-5 credits by demand)

ANTI-GAMING MEASURES
  • → Credits don't carry over (use it or lose it)
  • → Can't trade or transfer credits
  • → Account-level tracking prevents alt accounts
  • → Reputation affects future allocations

The Same Principles Apply to AI Systems

Judgment routing, decision receipts, and authority delegation for autonomous AI use the same architectural patterns we've proven in social infrastructure.

  • → Portable reputation

AI Systems (Occupant)
  • → Decision receipts
  • → Cross-system audit trails
  • → Agent authority chains

  • → Escalation triggers
  • → Actions → accountability

The throughline: Systems design determines who bears costs and how those costs are distributed. Making invisible costs legible enables better governance and accountability—whether for social platforms or AI systems.
